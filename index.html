§ 01 · Origins

How I ended up here.

On values &
figuring it out

I grew up watching my parents build businesses from scratch. My dad's computer shop, stacked with motherboards and decades of returning customers. My mom's one-room preschool that grew into a three-teacher school with a farm and a waiting list.

Watching them carve their own paths shaped how I work: hard work is the baseline, you own your outcomes, and the only way to build something meaningful is by doing work you actually care about. I started working at 16 to help fund college — hostessing first, then moving into fine dining at Berkeley. That's where I learned to multitask, stay calm under pressure, read a room, and put the customer first even when you're under water. Those instincts still show up in how I sell.

I didn't always imagine myself having a career in tech. At UC Berkeley I studied Global Studies with a focus on poverty in Latin America. A fellowship took me on a three-month solo trip to South America. Ten-hour days on an agroecological farm in Colombia, then a school in Cusco using innovative teaching practices to lift children out of poverty. I was accepted into the Peace Corps in Costa Rica and thought that was my next two years. What I took from it: humility, and the reminder that in the hardest circumstances, people are quietly inventing their own solutions. Sometimes the traditional playbook has to go out the window — you have to reinvent the wheel.

That lesson stuck. A conversation with my dad changed my path — I realized what I actually wanted was autonomy, ownership, and the chance to build something from zero and watch it work. That took me to startups.
